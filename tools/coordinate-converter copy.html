Coordinate Converter

What This Tool Does

Coordinates come in many formats depending on who recorded them and what system they were using — and converting between them manually is error-prone and slow. This free coordinate converter handles every major format used in the field: Decimal Degrees (DD), the standard used by Google Maps and most digital platforms; Degrees Minutes Seconds (DMS), the traditional format used in paper maps and official documents; Degrees Decimal Minutes (DDM), used in aviation and marine navigation; MGRS (Military Grid Reference System), the NATO standard used by military and defense; UTM (Universal Transverse Mercator), the metric grid system used by surveyors and geographers; USNG (United States National Grid), the civilian equivalent of MGRS used by US emergency services; and what3words, the three-word address system increasingly used by journalists, field investigators, and emergency responders. Enter coordinates in any format and instantly receive all others — no manual calculation, no errors. Every conversion is performed in real time with full precision. The tool also supports reverse what3words lookup — paste a three-word address like ///filled.count.soap and instantly receive the precise GPS coordinates, MGRS grid reference, and all other formats. This makes it the only free tool that converts bidirectionally between what3words and every major coordinate standard. Essential for OSINT investigators geolocating field reports, journalists pinpointing locations from sources, search and rescue coordinators, military analysts working with MGRS grids, surveyors and GIS professionals, and anyone who needs to move coordinates between systems without error. Supports the WGS84 datum used globally by GPS systems.
