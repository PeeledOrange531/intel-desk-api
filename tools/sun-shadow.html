Sun & Shadow Angle Calculator

What This Tool Does

The position of the sun in the sky is mathematically deterministic — given any location on Earth and any moment in time, the sun's exact azimuth and elevation can be calculated to within fractions of a degree. This tool uses that principle for photo timestamp verification. Enter a photograph's claimed coordinates, date, and time, and this calculator returns the sun's precise azimuth (compass bearing), elevation angle (height above horizon), shadow direction (opposite azimuth), and shadow length ratio (shadow length ÷ object height). These values can be compared against the shadows visible in a photograph to confirm or disprove its claimed timestamp. A shadow pointing northwest means the sun was in the southeast — if the claimed time and location puts the sun somewhere else, the timestamp is false. Also calculates sunrise, solar noon, and sunset times for the given coordinates and date, as well as golden hour windows. Used by journalists verifying conflict imagery, investigators authenticating evidence photographs, intelligence analysts geolocating images, and forensic researchers.
