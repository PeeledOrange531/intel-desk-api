What This Tool Does

Every email carries a hidden trail — a chain of technical metadata embedded in its headers that reveals exactly where it came from, which servers handled it, and whether its sender identity is genuine. This free email header analyzer decodes that trail instantly. Paste the raw headers from any email and this tool will trace the full server hop chain, extract and surface originating IP addresses, decode timestamps, and evaluate email authentication results including SPF, DKIM, and DMARC — the three standards used to detect spoofing and impersonation. It will flag anomalies like mismatched sender domains, forged display names, reply-to hijacking, and signs of phishing or business email compromise (BEC). Whether you are a journalist verifying a source, a security researcher investigating a phishing campaign, a fraud investigator tracing a scam email, or an IT professional diagnosing mail delivery failures, this tool gives you a clear, plain-language breakdown of everything hidden inside an email's headers — no command line required.
